\subsection{handling tables}

In \LaTeX , tables are handled by   the command \texttt{tabular} as follow :

\begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|}
% hline draw vertical line
\hline
A & B & C\\
\hline
\hline
$a_1$ & $b_2$ & $c_1$\\
\hline
$a_3$ & $b_2$ & $c_3$\\
\hline
$a_2$ & $b_1$ & $c_3$\\
\hline
\end{tabular}


\medskip

more complex table

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{|c||c|c|}
\hline
A & B & C\\
\hline
\hline
\multirow{3}{5mm}{$a_1$}
& $b_2$ & $c_1$\\
% 
\cline{2-3}
& \multicolumn{2}{c|}{$b_2$ et $c_3$}\\
\cline{2-3}
& $b_1$ & $c_3$\\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\subsection{maths}

In \LaTeX, the formula are within  ``dollar'' as follow : $a.x + b = 0$, $x_0$, $y^{12}$, $\sum_{i=0}^{n}{f(i)}$ \ldots
Or :
$$f(x) = \min_{h \in H}{h(\frac{n}{x})}$$
et numérotées comme suit :
\begin{center}
\begin{equation}
\label{formule1}
g(z)=\frac{1}{2}f(z) + 1
\end{equation}
\end{center}

this allows equation referencing  \ref{formule1}.
